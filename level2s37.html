7

Reading

Read the engaging dialogue below and identify how follow-up questions flow naturally.

At the Cafe

Anna: Hi Tom! How was your weekend?
Tom: It was great. I went to the mountains.
Anna: Wow! Who did you go with?
Tom: I went with my brother, Mark. We stayed in a small wooden cabin near a big lake. It was very beautiful and quiet.
Anna: Really? Was it cold at night?
Tom: Yes, it was freezing! We didn't have a heater, so we made a fire outside. We cooked hotdogs and marshmallows over the fire.
Anna: That sounds amazing! What did you do on Sunday morning?
Tom: We woke up very early, around 5:00 AM, and we went hiking up the mountain.
Anna: Oh wow! Why did you wake up so early?
Tom: Because we wanted to see the sunrise from the top of the mountain. It took two hours to climb to the top.
Anna: Two hours? Was it difficult?
Tom: Yes, it was very hard because the path was steep and rocky. But when we reached the top, the view was incredible. We saw the whole valley and the lake below us.
Anna: That is wonderful. Did you take any photos?
Tom: Yes, I took a lot of photos with my new camera. But then, something crazy happened on the way down. We saw a large black bear in the forest!
Anna: Oh my god! What did you do? Were you scared?
Tom: Yes, we were terrified! We stayed completely still and very quiet. We didn't run because that is dangerous. The bear just looked at us for a minute and then walked away into the trees.
Anna: Wow! That is a very scary story. What happened next?
Tom: After the bear left, we walked down the mountain very quickly. We packed our bags and drove back to the city. I was so happy to be home in my safe apartment.
Anna: I understand! I prefer the city to the mountains because there are no bears here!

Questions:

1. What is Anna's first follow-up question after Tom says he went to the mountains?
2. Who did Tom go with?
3. What does Anna ask after Tom mentions the cabin?
4. What does Anna ask when Tom says he saw a bear?
5. How did the follow-up questions help the conversation?
