Writing • Level 7

Mastery Writing

Execute flawless rhetorical structures. Practice meta-discourse framing, anticipating objections, spinning PR crises, and drafting legally binding ultimatums.

💡 10 Writing Topics

Choose a topic below and write a highly structured, persuasive paragraph (12-15 sentences) using Level 7 syntax.

1. Structural Overview: Write an introduction using meta-discourse ('My overarching objective is to delineate...').
2. The Grand Framing: Frame a complex debate ('The crux of the matter is not X, but rather Y').
3. The Lethal Pre-buttal: Anticipate and destroy an argument ('Detractors will inevitably argue...').
4. Systemic Diagnosis: Diagnose a global issue ('The fundamental flaw lies in systemic inequities').
5. The Legal Ultimatum: Draft a formal threat ('Failure to comply will render this null and void').
6. The Graceful Recovery: Apologize for a faux pas ('Please forgive the inadvertent mischaracterization').
7. The Academic Synthesis: Combine two views ('Synthesizing these disparate perspectives reveals...').
8. Macro Forecasting: Predict a future outcome ('This will inevitably culminate in a paradigm shift').
9. The Call to Action: End a speech powerfully ('I implore this committee to ratify this measure').
10. The Media Soundbite: Write a punchy antithesis quote ('We are not just surviving; we are thriving').

🎙️ PR Crisis Spin

Transform disastrous, honest facts into masterful, sanitized PR statements using agentless passives and distancing vocabulary.

"Our CEO stole millions."
→
"Our product explodes randomly."
→
"We are firing half the staff."
→
"We messed up the apology."
→

⚖️ Debate Outline

Construct a watertight debate opening utilizing framing and pre-buttals.

Resolution: Automation and AI will ultimately benefit human society.

  • Sentence 1: Frame the premise (The crux of the matter is...).
  • Sentence 2: Introduce the opponent's strongest point (Detractors will inevitably argue that...).
  • Sentence 3: Destroy that point (What this perspective fails to grasp, however, is...).
  • Sentence 4: Conclude with a deductive statement (Given the empirical evidence, it naturally follows that...).
