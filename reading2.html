Reading • Level 2

Elementary Reading

Expand your reading comprehension. Practice past and future tenses, understand frequency, identify comparatives, and extract the main ideas from short stories.

📖 Read & Answer

"Yesterday was Saturday. I woke up at 8 o'clock. I usually eat bread, but yesterday I ate pancakes. The weather was hotter than Friday. I went to the park and played football with my friends. Tomorrow, I am going to study English because I have a test."

✅ True or False

"Sarah wants to buy a new car. Her old car is very slow, and she prefers fast cars. She is going to visit the store next week. A blue car costs $10,000, and a red car costs $15,000. She will probably buy the blue car because it is cheaper."
1. Sarah's old car is very fast.
2. She is going to the store tomorrow.
3. The red car is more expensive than the blue car.
4. Sarah thinks the blue car is better because of the price.
5. She has already bought the car.

🔍 Vocabulary in Context

Read the sentence and guess the meaning of the underlined word.

1. I always brush my teeth before sleeping. 7 days a week.

2. My brother is very tall, but I am short.

3. The book costs 15 dollars.

4. I am tired, so I will go to bed.

5. She is going to study at university next year.

📑 Sequencing

Read the sentences about a trip. Put them in the correct chronological order (1, 2, 3, 4).

A. We went to the beach and swam in the ocean.
B. After swimming, we ate a big dinner at a restaurant.
C. Last weekend, my family drove to Florida.
D. Finally, we drove back home on Sunday night.

💡 Main Idea & Inference

"Many people think winter is the best season, but I disagree. I think summer is better because the weather is hot and sunny. In winter, I am always cold, so I stay inside. In summer, I can go to the beach, play sports outside, and wear shorts. Summer is definitely more fun than winter."
