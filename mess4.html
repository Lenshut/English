LEVEL 4: INTERMEDIATE

Evaluator Measurement & Placement Protocol

I. Speaking (5 Prompts)

Instructions to Evaluator: Do not prompt or translate. Assess immediate verbal reaction.

1. Prompt: "Tell me about a hobby. How long have you been doing it?"
2. Prompt: "Ask me politely what time the meeting starts without using a direct question."
3. Prompt: "If you had a million dollars, what would you buy?"
4. Prompt: "I am talking too much in a meeting. Interrupt me politely to add a point."
5. Prompt: "Imagine you are looking at a line graph going up very fast. Describe it using a verb and an adverb."
📖

II. Reading (5 Questions)

"Although the company faced difficulties last year, we have been growing steadily since January. Due to the hard work of our team, who dedicated endless hours, we avoided bankruptcy. If we had not changed our marketing strategy, we would have failed. Therefore, it is clear that adaptability is our greatest asset. Moving on to the next quarter, we expect this trend to continue."

6. Did the company fail last year?

7. How long has the company been growing?

8. What conditional grammar is used in "If we had not changed... we would have failed"?

9. According to the text, what is the specific cause of avoiding bankruptcy?

10. What transition phrase is used to shift the topic to the future?

✍️

III. Writing (5 Questions)

Write the correct sentence based on the instructions:

11. Unscramble: know / where / you / the / is / station / Do / ?

12. Third Conditional: If I (wake up) earlier, I (catch) the train.

13. Combine using 'Although': It was raining. We went to the beach.

14. Choose Formal Cause: The game was cancelled (because of / due to) the rain.

15. Hedging: Rephrase the absolute statement "Dogs are friendly" using a verb of tendency.

ANSWER KEY

I. Speaking

  1. "I have been doing [hobby] for [time]." (Must use 'have been -ing').
  2. "Could you tell me what time the meeting starts?" (Embedded question rule: subject before verb).
  3. "If I had a million dollars, I would buy..." (2nd conditional: past tense + would).
  4. "Sorry to interrupt, but if I could just add..."
  5. "Sales increased significantly." (Verb + Adverb).

II. Reading

  1. No, they faced difficulties but avoided bankruptcy.
  2. Since January.
  3. Third Conditional.
  4. The hard work of the team / dedicating endless hours.
  5. "Moving on to..."

III. Writing

  1. Do you know where the station is?
  2. If I had woken up earlier, I would have caught the train.
  3. Although it was raining, we went to the beach.
  4. due to (followed by a noun phrase).
  5. E.g., Dogs tend to be friendly. / Generally, dogs are friendly.

MEASUREMENT CONSIDERATION

Do not rely solely on the written scores. The speaking execution dictates the final placement.

Belongs in Level 3 (-1 Level)

If the student fails to shift into unreal past for the 2nd conditional ("If I have money, I will buy") or inverts embedded questions ("Do you know where is it?"), their grasp of complex syntax is fragile. Demote to Level 3 to establish foundational modal control and basic opinions.

Belongs in Level 4 (0 Level | Baseline)

Understands the rules of "have been -ing" and embedded clauses, but applies them slowly. They can read the third conditional text but might stumble trying to generate it verbally. They belong in Level 4 to harden these advanced academic and professional structures.

Belongs in Level 5 (+1 Level)

Executes the subjunctive "were" in conditionals flawlessly. They effortlessly use "Although" and describe graphs with accurate verb-adverb pairings. Their speech sounds semi-professional automatically. Move to Level 5 to challenge them with de-escalation, corporate jargon, and negative inversion.
