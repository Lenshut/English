6

Speaking Tasks

Practice contrasting your preferences aloud to a partner.

Task A: Read Aloud

"I like to read books, but I do not like to write."

Task B: Read Aloud

"The food is very good, but it is too expensive."

Task C: Read Aloud

"I want to go to the park today, but it is raining outside."

Task D: Teacher Roleplay
Teacher:
"Let's order dinner! Do you like pizza and burgers?"
You:
"I like pizza, but I do not like burgers."
Teacher:
"Okay! Do you drink coffee or tea?"
You:
"I drink tea, but I do not drink coffee. It is too bitter."
7

Reading

Read the passage below and answer the questions highlighting the contrasting ideas.

David lives in a new house. The house is very big, but it is old. David likes the garden, but he does not like the kitchen. The kitchen is small and dark.

David has a pet. He has a large dog, but he does not have a cat. He likes dogs because they play, but he thinks cats are lazy.

On the weekend, David likes to stay active. He plays tennis and basketball, but he does not play football. He likes summer because it is sunny, but he does not like winter because it is cold. He wants to travel to Spain, but he does not have the time right now.

Questions:

1. David's house is big, but what is the problem with it?
2. What room does David not like?
3. David has a dog, but what animal does he not have?
4. What sport does David not play?
5. David wants to travel to Spain, but why can't he go?
