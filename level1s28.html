Speaking Tasks

Read the texts aloud clearly. Emphasize the 'S' sound on the verbs! Then, roleplay.

Task A: Read Aloud

"I read a book, but he reads a book too. She writes a letter."

Task B: Read Aloud

"My mother goes to work. My father works in the store."

Task C: Read Aloud

"The dog plays in the park. It runs fast and it drinks water."

Task D: Teacher Roleplay
Teacher:
"What do you do in the morning?"
You:
"I wake up and I eat food."
Teacher:
"What does your brother do?"
You:
"He wakes up and he drinks cold water. He plays with the dog."
7

Reading

Read the story about Tom and answer the questions below. Notice the 's' on the verbs!

Hello! My name is Lily, and I want to tell you about my brother. His name is Tom. Tom is a very busy boy. In the morning, he wakes up early. He wakes up at six o'clock. He goes to the bathroom and he washes his face. Then, he goes to the kitchen. He eats a big breakfast. He eats eggs and bread. He drinks cold milk. He does not drink hot tea.

After breakfast, Tom goes to school. He walks to school with his friends. At school, he studies English and math. He reads big books and he writes long letters. He likes his school. The teacher talks, and Tom listens carefully.

In the afternoon, Tom plays in the park. He plays football with his dog. The dog runs fast and catches the ball. Tom laughs and runs with the dog. He drinks water because he is hot.

In the evening, Tom goes home. He helps our mother in the kitchen. He eats a hot dinner with the family. After dinner, he reads a small book in his bedroom. He does not play games at night. He is very tired. Finally, he sleeps in his big bed. He sleeps very well. Tomorrow, he wakes up and he does it all again. Tom has a very good routine.

Questions:

1. What does Tom do in the morning?
2. What does he drink?
3. Where does Tom go after breakfast?
4. What does the dog do in the park?
5. Where does Tom sleep at night?
