Speaking • Level 3

Pre-Intermediate Speaking

Develop narrative and persuasive flow. Tell multi-tense stories, express strong opinions with evidence, give advice, handle complaints, and deliver your first 1-minute monologue.

📖 Narrative Storytelling

Combine Past Continuous (background) and Past Simple (interruption) to tell a dramatic story aloud.

Prompt 1: The Broken Phone

Start with: "I was walking down the street when suddenly..." Explain how you broke your phone and how you felt.

Prompt 2: The Bad Weather

Start with: "We were playing football in the park when..." Explain what happened with the weather and what you did in the end.

🗣️ Expressing Opinions

State your opinion strongly and provide at least ONE concrete example using "For example" or "Such as".

Debate 1: Are smartphones bad for children? (Start: "I strongly believe...")
Debate 2: Is it better to study at home or in school? (Start: "In my opinion...")
Debate 3: Your partner says fast food is great. Disagree politely (Start: "I see your point, but...")

💡 Giving Advice

Your partner has a problem. Give them advice using 'should', 'must', or 'If I were you'.

🤒

Problem: "I feel terrible. I have a headache and a fever."

Action: Tell them 2 things they should do and 1 thing they must not do.

💼

Problem: "I hate my job. My boss is very angry."

Action: Give advice starting with "If I were you..."

🛠️ Problem Solving & Apologies

Handle these difficult social situations politely but effectively.

Scenario A: The Apology

You are 30 minutes late to meet your friend. Apologize and give a past-tense reason why.

Use: "I am so sorry, I meant to..."

Scenario B: The Return

You bought a jacket, but there is a hole in it. Go to the cashier and return it politely.

Use: "I would like to return this because..."

⏱️ 1-Minute Monologue

You have 1 minute to prepare. Speak continuously for 60 seconds on the topic below.

Topic: "My 5-Year Plan"

Talk about your life in the future. Include:

  • Where you hope to live.
  • What you plan to do for work.
  • Use 1st Conditional (If I get a good job, I will...)
  • Use probability (I will definitely / I will probably)
