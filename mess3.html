PLACEMENT TEST : LEVEL 3

LEVEL 3: PRE-INTERMEDIATE

Evaluator Measurement & Placement Protocol

🗣️

I. Speaking (5 Prompts)

Instructions to Evaluator: Do not prompt or translate. Assess immediate verbal reaction.

1. Prompt: "Tell me a short story about yesterday using 'First, then, and finally'."
2. Prompt: "I was walking to the store when... (finish the sentence with a sudden interruption)."
3. Prompt: "I think winter is the best season. Politely disagree with me and give an example why."
4. Prompt: "If it rains tomorrow, what will you do?"
5. Prompt: "Compare a bicycle and a car using at least two different comparative adjectives."
📖

II. Reading (5 Questions)

"When I was young, I used to play the piano every day. I strongly believe that music is important for children. For example, it helps with memory. If I have children in the future, I will definitely teach them an instrument. I think the piano is more difficult than the guitar, but it is the most beautiful instrument."

6. Did the writer play the piano recently?

7. What is one example of why music is important?

8. What is the writer's future plan regarding children?

9. Which instrument does the writer think is harder?

10. What superlative is used in the text to describe the piano?

✍️

III. Writing (5 Questions)

Write the correct sentence based on the instructions:

11. Unscramble: playing / when / rang / I / phone / was / the / games / .

12. Add a comparative: A dog is (fast) than a turtle.

13. Make a polite request: (Give me water.)

14. 1st Conditional: If I (study), I (pass) the test.

15. Give advice: "I am always tired." -> You should...

🧩

IV. Grammar (5 Questions)

16. I was reading a book ___ the lights went out.

  • a) while
  • b) when
  • c) so

17. You ___ wear a seatbelt. It's the law.

  • a) should
  • b) could
  • c) must

18. This is the ___ expensive car here.

  • a) more
  • b) most
  • c) much

19. If she ___, we will go to the park.

  • a) will come
  • b) comes
  • c) came

20. I ___ to eat meat, but now I am a vegetarian.

  • a) use
  • b) used
  • c) am used
📚

V. Vocabulary (5 Questions)

  • a) Can I
  • b) Could I

  • a) Baddest
  • b) Worse
  • c) Worst

23. To politely disagree, you say:

  • a) You are wrong
  • b) I see your point, but
  • c) I am not agree

24. "Definitely" means:

  • a) 50% sure
  • b) 100% sure
  • c) 0% sure

25. To keep a conversation going, you ask a:

  • a) Follow-up question
  • b) Conditional
  • c) Superlative

ANSWER KEY

I. Speaking

  1. Fluid 3-part sequence. "First I... then I... and finally I..."
  2. "...when [Past Simple Interruption]." (e.g., when a dog barked).
  3. "I see your point, but I prefer summer. For example, I can swim."
  4. "If it rains, I will [base verb]." (No double will).
  5. "A car is faster than a bicycle, and it is more expensive." (Must use both -er and more).

II. Reading

  1. No, "used to" means past habit.
  2. It helps with memory.
  3. He will teach them an instrument.
  4. The piano.
  5. "the most beautiful".

III. Writing

  1. I was playing games when the phone rang.
  2. A dog is faster than a turtle.
  3. Could I have water, please?
  4. If I study, I will pass the test.
  5. You should sleep.

IV. Grammar & V. Vocab

  • 16. b (when)
  • 21. b (Could I)
  • 17. c (must)
  • 22. b (Worse)
  • 18. b (most)
  • 23. b (I see your point...)
  • 19. b (comes)
  • 24. b (100% sure)
  • 20. b (used)
  • 25. a (Follow-up)

MEASUREMENT CONSIDERATION

Do not rely solely on the written scores. The speaking execution dictates the final placement.

Belongs in Level 2 (-1 Level)

Struggles with the continuous vs simple interruption ("I was walk when he call"). Cannot form 1st conditionals without double willing ("If it will rain, I will go"). Demote to Level 2 to harden their basic tenses before attempting complex storytelling.

Belongs in Level 3 (0 Level | Baseline)

Can formulate the comparative sentences and conditionals but speaks slowly, pausing to calculate the grammar. They understand "used to" and polite phrasing but lack the fluidity required for structured academic debate. Keep in Level 3.

Belongs in Level 4 (+1 Level)

Flawlessly connects "was -ing" with "when -ed". Delivers comparisons and conditionals instantly. They actively try to extend the conversation rather than just answering. Move to Level 4 to challenge them with present perfect continuous and professional presentation structures.
