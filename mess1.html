PLACEMENT TEST : LEVEL 1

LEVEL 1: FOUNDATION

Evaluator Measurement & Placement Protocol

🗣️

I. Speaking (5 Prompts)

Instructions to Evaluator: Do not prompt or translate. Assess immediate verbal reaction.

1. Prompt: "Hello. Introduce yourself, tell me your age, and spell your first name out loud."
2. Prompt: Point to a blue chair. "What is this, and what color is it?"
3. Prompt: "Tell me your morning routine in three steps."
4. Prompt: "I am the waiter. You are hungry and do not like onions. Order your food."
5. Prompt: "Ask me where the hospital is, and ask me how much a ticket costs."
📖

II. Reading (5 Questions)

"My name is Sarah. I am 25 years old. I wake up at 6 o'clock every day. I usually eat bread, but I do not like milk. I go to work at 8 o'clock. My office is next to the bank and across from the park."

6. How old is Sarah?

7. What time does she wake up?

8. Does she like milk?

9. Where does she go at 8 o'clock?

10. Is the office under the bank?

✍️

III. Writing (5 Questions)

Write the correct sentence based on the instructions:

11. Unscramble: name / My / John / is / .

12. Make Negative: She is a teacher.

13. Pluralize: I have one apple. (Change 'one' to 'four').

14. Add the 'S' Rule: He (go) to the store.

15. Answer the question (Full Sentence): Do you like fish?

🧩

IV. Grammar (5 Questions)

16. She ___ a girl.

  • a) am
  • b) is
  • c) are

17. Is this ___ eraser?

  • a) a
  • b) an
  • c) the

18. ___ you like water?

  • a) Are
  • b) Do
  • c) Is

19. The cat is ___ the desk. (Sitting on flat surface).

  • a) in
  • b) on
  • c) under

20. ___ is that man? He is my father.

  • a) Who
  • b) What
  • c) Where
📚

V. Vocabulary (5 Questions)

21. What comes after "Six"?

  • a) Four
  • b) Seven
  • c) Nine

22. "Turn left" and "Go straight" are examples of:

  • a) Emotions
  • b) Directions
  • c) Clothes

23. The opposite of "Hot" is:

  • a) Fast
  • b) Sad
  • c) Cold

24. If I say "Ten dollars," I am talking about:

  • a) Price
  • b) Time
  • c) Age

25. "Happy", "Sad", and "Angry" are:

  • a) Verbs
  • b) Feelings
  • c) Family

ANSWER KEY

I. Speaking

  1. Must state name, use "I am [number]", and spell name letter-by-letter.
  2. "It is a chair. It is blue." (Must use 'a' for the noun, no 'a' for the color).
  3. E.g., "I wake up. I eat. I go to work." (Must use base present simple verbs with 'I').
  4. E.g., "I want a burger, but no onions, please."
  5. "Where is the hospital? How much is the ticket?"

II. Reading

  1. She is 25 years old.
  2. She wakes up at 6 o'clock.
  3. No, she does not.
  4. She goes to work.
  5. No, it is next to the bank.

III. Writing

  1. My name is John.
  2. She is not a teacher.
  3. I have four apples. (Must include 's').
  4. He goes to the store. (Must use 'es').
  5. Yes, I do. / No, I do not.

IV. Grammar & V. Vocab

  • 16. b (is)
  • 21. b (Seven)
  • 17. b (an)
  • 22. b (Directions)
  • 18. b (Do)
  • 23. c (Cold)
  • 19. b (on)
  • 24. a (Price)
  • 20. a (Who)
  • 25. b (Feelings)

MEASUREMENT CONSIDERATION

Do not rely solely on the written scores. The speaking execution dictates the final placement.

Belongs in Level 1 (0–15 points / Speaking breakdown)

The student hesitates heavily. They drop the 'to be' verb ("I happy"), forget the 3rd person 's' ("He go"), or cannot formulate basic WH-questions or 'Do' questions. They need foundational drilling.

Belongs in Level 2 (+1 Level | 16–22 points / Adequate Speaking)

The student passes this test easily. They can state routines, use 'do you like', and navigate directions. However, their sentences are isolated. They do not naturally use 'and', 'but', or 'because' to connect thoughts. Put them in Level 2 to learn paragraph building and past tense.

Belongs in Level 3 (+2 Levels | 23–25 points / Fluid Speaking)

The student not only answers perfectly but uses past tense naturally during the speaking test (e.g., "Yesterday I went..."). They use conjunctions without prompting. Level 1 and 2 will bore them; move them to Level 3 to test their hypothetical and storytelling mechanics.

Lower than Level 1 (-1 Level)

N/A. Level 1 is designed for absolute zero beginners. If they score a 0, this is exactly where they belong.
