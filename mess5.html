PLACEMENT TEST : LEVEL 5

LEVEL 5: UPPER-INTERMEDIATE

Evaluator Measurement & Placement Protocol

🗣️

I. Speaking (5 Prompts)

Instructions to Evaluator: Do not prompt or translate. Assess immediate verbal reaction.

1. Prompt: "Explain what you have been doing at work or school recently, and contrast it with a completed achievement."
2. Prompt: "Imagine you failed an important exam last year. Tell me how the past would have been different if you had studied."
3. Prompt: "I am an angry client demanding a full refund. Deflect my anger politely and offer a compromise."
4. Prompt: "Interrupt me politely and redirect this conversation back to the main agenda."
5. Prompt: "Play Devil's Advocate against the idea that working from home is perfectly efficient."
📖

II. Reading (5 Questions)

"We have been negotiating this contract for three months. If the client had provided the data earlier, we would have launched in May. Be that as it may, we must proceed. I understand your hesitation, but the fact of the matter is that further delays are unacceptable. I am willing to offer a minor discount to finalize this today. Let's hash this out before we wrap up the meeting."

6. How long has the negotiation been ongoing?

7. What prevented the May launch?

8. What phrase is used to pivot past the bad news?

9. What compromise is the speaker offering?

10. What does the speaker want to do before the meeting ends ("wrap up")?

✍️

III. Writing (5 Questions)

Write the correct sentence based on the instructions:

11. Unscramble: matter / the / fact / is / of / out of / we / are / time / the / .

12. Third Conditional: If they (listen) to the warnings, the crisis (be) avoided.

13. Cleft Sentence: Focus on the verb: I really *need* a vacation. -> What I really need...

14. Participle Clause: Compress: "Because he realized he was late, John ran."

15. Add a Tag Question: You haven't sent the email yet, ___?

🧩

IV. Grammar (5 Questions)

16. I have ___ waiting here since 9 AM.

  • a) am
  • b) been
  • c) being

17. ___ I had the money, I would have bought it.

  • a) If
  • b) Unless
  • c) Whether

18. We will finish on time, ___ that there are no delays.

  • a) unless
  • b) provided
  • c) assuming

19. ___ the car was expensive, it frequently broke down.

  • a) Despite
  • b) However
  • c) Although

20. Just because he is loud, ___ mean he is right.

  • a) it doesn't necessarily
  • b) so it doesn't
  • c) that doesn't necessary

ANSWER KEY

I. Speaking

  1. "I have been [V-ing]... and I have [V3]..." (Must distinguish PPC and PPS).
  2. "If I had studied, I would have passed." (Had + V3, Would have + V3).
  3. "I understand your frustration. I am willing to offer..." (Validate + Compromise).
  4. "Sorry to interject, but let's circle back to the agenda."
  5. "Let me play devil's advocate. What if the internet goes down?"

II. Reading

  1. For three months.
  2. The client did not provide the data earlier.
  3. "Be that as it may".
  4. A minor discount.
  5. Hash it out (resolve the issue).

III. Writing

  1. The fact of the matter is we are out of time.
  2. If they had listened to the warnings, the crisis would have been avoided.
  3. What I really need is a vacation.
  4. Realizing he was late, John ran.
  5. have you?

MEASUREMENT CONSIDERATION

Do not rely solely on the written scores. The speaking execution dictates the final placement.

Belongs in Level 4 (-1 Level)

If the student fails to grasp the 3rd conditional timeline ("If I study, I would passed") or struggles to separate "I have been doing" from "I have done", their upper-intermediate mechanics are failing under load. Demote to Level 4 to harden complex clauses and basic conditional logic.

Belongs in Level 5 (0 Level | Baseline)

Executes the 3rd conditional correctly and uses "willing to" or "circle back" functionally, but still relies heavily on standard transitions (but, so). They understand the concepts but lack C1-level diplomatic nuance and rhetorical polish. Keep in Level 5.

Belongs in Level 6 (+1 Level)

Effortlessly utilizes "Be that as it may" to pivot, seamlessly handles the "What-cleft", and modulates their tone perfectly during the de-escalation prompt without sounding aggressive. They are ready for Advanced executive grammar and hedging. Move to Level 6.
