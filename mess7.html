PLACEMENT TEST : LEVEL 7

LEVEL 7: MASTERY

Evaluator Measurement & Placement Protocol

🗣️

I. Speaking (5 Prompts)

Instructions to Evaluator: Do not prompt or translate. Assess immediate verbal reaction.

1. Prompt: "Define 'Freedom' using the 'Not merely X, but rather Y' abstract framework."
2. Prompt: "Deliver a pre-buttal for a proposal to replace all teachers with AI."
3. Prompt: "Diagnose a systemic failure in modern corporate culture using 'The fundamental flaw lies in...'."
4. Prompt: "Analyze a risky business merger by weighing its synergy against its friction."
5. Prompt: "You are a CEO facing a massive data breach. Enact damage control and spin the narrative."
📖

II. Reading (5 Questions)

"While globalization fosters economic integration, it simultaneously threatens to dilute indigenous heritage. Critics may argue that this homogenization is an inevitable consequence of progress; however, turning a blind eye to this cultural erosion is tantamount to complicity. Given the empirical evidence, it naturally follows that damage control protocols must be enacted to preserve these identities."

6. What is the "double-edged sword" effect of globalization described here?

7. Identify the exact pre-buttal used in the text.

8. What does "tantamount to complicity" mean regarding cultural erosion?

9. What deductive logic phrase connects the evidence to the conclusion?

10. Are the damage control protocols presented as a suggestion or a mandate?

✍️

III. Writing (5 Questions)

Write the correct sentence based on the instructions:

11. Abstract Definition: Success is not merely money, but rather [complete the thought].

12. Absolute Concession: Even if we assume your plan works, [dismantle it].

13. Deductive Logic: Given the low sales, it naturally follows that [conclusion].

14. Legal Termination: Pursuant to the contract, this breach renders the agreement [legal term].

15. Project Phase: Phase one entails [restructure the team].

🧩

ANSWER KEY

I. Speaking

  1. "Freedom is not merely [noun], but rather [noun phrase]."
  2. "Some critics may argue that AI is cheaper; however, it lacks human empathy."
  3. "The fundamental flaw lies in [noun phrase]."
  4. "The synergy is [X], but the friction could derail it."
  5. "This was an anomaly. We are enacting damage control and overhauling security."

II. Reading

  1. Fosters integration vs dilutes heritage.
  2. "Critics may argue... however..."
  3. Turning a blind eye is equal to guilt/participation in the erosion.
  4. "Given the empirical evidence, it naturally follows that..."
  5. A mandate ("must be enacted", passive voice).

III. Writing

  1. ...but rather the pursuit of happiness (or similar).
  2. ...it remains fundamentally inadequate.
  3. ...we must restructure our approach.
  4. ...null and void.
  5. ...entails restructuring the team (Must use gerund).

IV. Grammar & V. Vocab

  • 16. b (in)
  • 21. a (Negligible)
  • 17. c (updating)
  • 22. a (Retaliatory)
  • 18. a (Even if)
  • 23. b (Intrinsic)
  • 19. a (to)
  • 24. a (Dilute)
  • 20. b (thereby)
  • 25. a (Paradigm shift)

MEASUREMENT CONSIDERATION

Do not rely solely on the written scores. The speaking execution dictates the final placement.

Belongs in Level 6 (-1 Level)

If the student cannot sustain abstract discussion ("Freedom is doing what I want") or fails the gerund requirement for project phases ("entails to fix"), their academic precision is lacking. Demote to Level 6 to polish their register switching and basic formal structures.

Belongs in Level 7 (0 Level | Baseline)

Successfully articulates abstract concepts using "Not merely... but rather" and applies "tantamount to" accurately. They understand deductive logic but may lack the ultimate commanding stage presence of a C2 speaker. Keep in Level 7 to refine their high-stakes performance and philosophy.

Belongs in Level 8 (+1 Level)

Executes damage control spin with terrifying corporate accuracy. Handles the pre-buttal flawlessly without hesitating. Their vocabulary is elite (negligible, retaliatory). Move them to Level 8 for the final C2 Capstone, Oxford Debates, and unscripted Socratic seminars.
