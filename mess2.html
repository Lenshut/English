PLACEMENT TEST : LEVEL 2

LEVEL 2: ELEMENTARY

Evaluator Measurement & Placement Protocol

🗣️

I. Speaking (5 Prompts)

Instructions to Evaluator: Do not prompt or translate. Assess immediate verbal reaction.

1. Prompt: "Combine these ideas into one sentence: I like tea. I do not like coffee. I drink tea every morning."
2. Prompt: "How often do you go to the cinema, and what did you watch last time?"
3. Prompt: "Ask me a question about what I did yesterday."
4. Prompt: "Tell me about a concrete plan you have for next year."
5. Prompt: "Do you prefer cats or dogs? Tell me why."
📖

II. Reading (5 Questions)

"Yesterday, I went to the park because the weather was beautiful. I usually stay home on Sundays, but my friends invited me. Next week, we are going to visit the museum. I think museums are interesting, but my brother disagrees."

6. Why did the writer go to the park?

7. What does the writer usually do on Sundays?

8. What are they going to do next week?

9. Does the writer think museums are boring?

10. Does the brother agree with the writer?

✍️

III. Writing (5 Questions)

Write the correct sentence based on the instructions:

11. Unscramble: usually / I / at / wake up / 7 AM / .

12. Make Negative: I went to the store.

13. Combine with a conjunction: I am tired. I did not sleep.

14. Answer (Full Sentence): Are you going to study tomorrow?

15. Ask a question to get this answer: (They went to London.)

🧩

IV. Grammar (5 Questions)

16. I ___ happy yesterday.

  • a) am
  • b) was
  • c) were

17. ___ you watch the movie last night?

  • a) Do
  • b) Are
  • c) Did

18. I am ___ to travel next year.

  • a) going
  • b) will
  • c) going to

19. I like apples, ___ I don't like bananas.

  • a) and
  • b) so
  • c) but

20. He is ___ late.

  • a) always
  • b) always is
  • c) always are
📚

V. Vocabulary (5 Questions)

21. The opposite of 'Always' is:

  • a) Sometimes
  • b) Never
  • c) Usually

22. If you have a solid, scheduled plan, you use:

  • a) Will
  • b) Going to
  • c) Might

23. What comes after "First":

  • a) Finally
  • b) Then
  • c) Because

24. "Went", "Ate", and "Saw" are:

  • a) Present verbs
  • b) Future verbs
  • c) Past irregular verbs

25. To express a subjective viewpoint, you start with:

  • a) I think
  • b) I do
  • c) I am

ANSWER KEY

I. Speaking

  1. "I like tea and I drink it every morning, but I do not like coffee." (Must use 'and' & 'but').
  2. E.g., "I usually go to the cinema. I watched [Movie]." (Adverb + Past tense).
  3. "Did you [verb] yesterday?" (Must use 'Did' + base verb).
  4. "I am going to [action]." (Must include 'am going to').
  5. "I prefer [X] because [reason]."

II. Reading

  1. Because the weather was beautiful.
  2. She usually stays home.
  3. They are going to visit the museum.
  4. No, she thinks they are interesting.
  5. No, he disagrees.

III. Writing

  1. I usually wake up at 7 AM.
  2. I did not go to the store. (Base verb required).
  3. I am tired because I did not sleep.
  4. Yes, I am going to study tomorrow. / No, I am not.
  5. Where did they go?

IV. Grammar & V. Vocab

  • 16. b (was)
  • 21. b (Never)
  • 17. c (Did)
  • 22. b (Going to)
  • 18. a (going)
  • 23. b (Then)
  • 19. c (but)
  • 24. c (Past irregular)
  • 20. a (always)
  • 25. a (I think)

MEASUREMENT CONSIDERATION

Do not rely solely on the written scores. The speaking execution dictates the final placement.

Belongs in Level 1 (-1 Level)

Fails the speaking mechanics immediately. Reverts to "I am go", fails to formulate past questions ("You went yesterday?"), or cannot string two clauses together. Their baseline is fundamentally broken. Demote to Level 1.

Belongs in Level 2 (0 Level | Baseline)

Can answer the prompts but shows hesitation with irregular verbs or question inversion ("Did you went?"). They understand the concepts but lack the fluid execution required to move into storytelling or continuous dialogue. Keep in Level 2 to harden the mechanics.

Belongs in Level 3 (+1 Level)

Handles past, future, and conjunctions perfectly without translating in their head. They justify opinions easily. Move to Level 3 to introduce continuous narrative storytelling and nuanced modal advice.
