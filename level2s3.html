Task D: Teacher Roleplay
Teacher:
"Why are you wearing a big jacket inside?"
You:
"I am wearing a jacket because I am cold."
Teacher:
"Ah, okay. Why are you eating a sandwich?"

"I am eating a sandwich because I am very hungry."
7

Reading

Read the passage below and extract the logical reasons behind the characters' actions.

Sarah has a very organized life, and she always has a good reason for everything she does. She goes to the supermarket every Monday because she needs fresh food for the week. She walks to the store because she does not have a car, and she likes the exercise. At the store, she buys many apples and oranges because they are very healthy for her body. She does not buy chocolate cake or candy because those foods have too much sugar. She wants to feel energetic, so she chooses her food carefully.

After shopping, Sarah goes straight home because she is very tired from walking. When she arrives, she drinks a big glass of cold water because it is hot outside, and she is very thirsty. In the evening, she opens the living room window because she wants fresh air in the house. She likes to sit near the window and read a book. She reads many books because they are interesting and they help her relax after a long day. She does not watch television because she thinks it is boring.

Later, Sarah calls her mother on the telephone because she lives in a different city. They talk for an hour because they have many things to share. Sarah smiles a lot during the phone call because she is happy to hear her mother's voice. Finally, she turns off the lights and goes to sleep early. She goes to bed at 9:00 PM because she has to wake up at 5:00 AM for work tomorrow. She needs a lot of sleep because her job is very difficult. Sarah's days are busy, but she is happy because she takes good care of herself.

Questions:

1. Why does Sarah walk to the store?
2. Why does she buy apples and oranges?
3. Why does she drink a big glass of cold water?
4. Why does she read many books in the evening?
5. Why does she go to sleep early at 9:00 PM?
