PLACEMENT TEST : LEVEL 6

LEVEL 6: ADVANCED

Evaluator Measurement & Placement Protocol

🗣️

I. Speaking (5 Prompts)

Instructions to Evaluator: Do not prompt or translate. Assess immediate verbal reaction.

1. Prompt: "Use negative inversion to emphasize this point: We lost money and we lost our best client."
2. Prompt: "Soften this absolute claim using double-hedging: 'AI will ruin the economy.'"
3. Prompt: "Deliver a 3-point argument using advanced sequencing adverbs (Primarily...)."
4. Prompt: "Give me constructive criticism on my presentation style using the 'room for growth' structure."
5. Prompt: "Respond to this hostile question: 'Your plan is a waste of time. Why should we do it?'"
📖

II. Reading (5 Questions)

"Not only did the initiative fail to meet projections, but it also alienated our core demographic. Had we anticipated the market shift, we could have mitigated the fallout. It appears to be the case that our strategy was overly optimistic. Should you require further clarification, please ascertain the details from the enclosed dossier. We must resolve this complication immediately."

6. What is the impact of the negative inversion in the first sentence?

7. What conditional structure is "Had we anticipated..." replacing?

8. How does the author distance themselves from the claim about the strategy?

9. What does "Should you require" mean in this context?

10. Translate "ascertain the details" to casual English.

✍️

III. Writing (5 Questions)

Write the correct sentence based on the instructions:

11. Invert: If I had known the risks, I would have stopped.

12. Soften with Diplomatic Request: Give me the file by Friday.

13. Translate to Formal: We need to start the meeting and figure out a plan.

14. Urgency Subjunctive: It is vital that the CEO (resigns/resign) today.

15. Deductive Logic: Connect these: Low budget -> Layoffs. (Given...)

🧩

ANSWER KEY

I. Speaking

  1. "Not only did we lose money, but we also lost our best client."
  2. "It might be argued that AI could potentially disrupt the economy."
  3. "Primarily... Subsequently... Ultimately..."
  4. "You excel at X, however there is room for growth regarding Y."
  5. "I understand your concern, but the data clearly indicates..."

II. Reading

  1. Extreme emphasis on the dual failures.
  2. If we had anticipated... (3rd conditional).
  3. "It appears to be the case that..."
  4. If you require / If you need.
  5. Find out / figure out the details.

III. Writing

  1. Had I known the risks, I would have stopped.
  2. I would appreciate it if you could send the file by Friday.
  3. We must commence the meeting and formulate a strategy.
  4. resign (Subjunctive: base verb, no 's').
  5. Given the low budget, it naturally follows that layoffs will occur.

  • 16. b (should you)

  • 17. a (Were he)
  • 22. b (Explain clearly)
  • 18. b (adhere)
  • 23. b (Possibility)
  • 19. a (Before)
  • 24. b (Massive change)
  • 20. b (is)
  • 25. c (Doubtful)

MEASUREMENT CONSIDERATION

Do not rely solely on the written scores. The speaking execution dictates the final placement.

Belongs in Level 5 (-1 Level)

If the student fails the negative inversion ("Not only we lost") or resorts to blunt, emotional defenses during the hostile Q&A ("No, the plan is good"), their executive polish is lacking. Demote to Level 5 to master basic debate tactics and corporate de-escalation.

Belongs in Level 6 (0 Level | Baseline)

Executes the subjunctive drop and inverted conditionals with some hesitation, but understands the logic. They can code-switch vocabulary but still sound slightly robotic. Keep in Level 6 to internalize hyper-formal and diplomatic linguistic patterns.

Belongs in Level 7 (+1 Level)

Handles negative inversion seamlessly and navigates the hostile Q&A with terrifying composure using "I understand your concern, but the data indicates". Ready for abstract philosophical argumentation, systemic analysis, and absolute concession. Move to Level 7.
