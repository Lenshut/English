Proficiency Reading

The pinnacle of reading comprehension. Test your ability to dissect profound academic theses, diagnose subtle cognitive biases, unravel paradoxes, and read between the lines of C-suite executive subtext.

📖 Read & Answer

"The central thesis of my inquiry posits that rapid hyper-urbanization invariably leads to societal fragmentation, necessitating a radical reevaluation of our civic infrastructure. While proponents argue that centralization fosters economic synergy, this perspective is fundamentally reductive. It completely ignores the ensuing erosion of intrinsic community bonds."

✅ True or False

"The board's decision to continue funding the defunct marketing initiative is a textbook manifestation of the sunk cost fallacy. Despite the quantitative metrics indicating a severe contraction, leadership remains ensnared in an echo chamber, predicated on the false equivalence that past investment guarantees future viability."
1. The board is continuing to fund a successful project.
2. The author believes the board is suffering from a specific cognitive bias.
3. The hard data supports the board's decision to keep funding.
4. The board mistakenly believes that spending money in the past ensures success later.

📑 Sequencing

B. Therefore, I am formally initiating an audit of all cross-functional departments to identify redundancies.

We are not experiencing stagnation; rather, we are undergoing a period of strategic consolidation before our next phase of aggressive expansion.'"
