Speaking • Level 1

Foundation Speaking

Build vocal confidence. Practice exact pronunciation, answer simple personal questions, describe basic pictures, and participate in essential survival roleplays.

🗣️ Pronunciation & Repetition

Listen to the teacher and repeat exactly. Focus on the underlined sounds.

Task 1: Vowels & Consonants

  • 1. Apple / Elephant
  • 2. Boy / Van
  • 3. Pen / Fan
  • 4. Three / Tree
  • 5. She / See

Task 2: Full Sentences

  • 1. Hello, my name is John.
  • 2. I am thirty years old.
  • 3. It is a red book.
  • 4. What is this?
  • 5. Yes, I am happy.

❓ Personal Q&A

Answer the teacher's questions out loud using full sentences. (No one-word answers!)

1. What is your name?
2. How old are you?
3. Are you a student or a teacher?
4. How are you today? (Are you happy, sad, tired?)
5. What is your favorite color?
6. Do you like pizza?
7. What time do you wake up?
8. Where are you from?

🖼️ Picture Description

Look at the items in the room or a picture the teacher shows you. Answer the questions.

🎒 🍎 🐕 🚌

Use these icons as practice prompts.

Prompt 1: Point to an object. Say: "This is a [color] [object]."
Prompt 2: Is the dog big or small? Say: "The dog is..."
Prompt 3: Where is the apple? (Use: In, On, Under)
Prompt 4: How many objects are there? Say: "I see [number] objects."

📖 Read Aloud

Read the following short paragraphs out loud clearly and slowly.

Passage A: Introduction

"Hello. My name is David. I am twenty years old. I am a student. I am happy today."

Passage B: My Room

"Look at my room. The desk is brown. The bag is red. The pen is on the desk. The dog is under the chair."

Passage C: Daily Routine

"I wake up at seven o'clock. I eat an apple. I drink water. I go to school. I like my school."

🎭 Survival Roleplay

Act out the following scenarios with the teacher or a partner.

Scenario 1: Meeting a Stranger

You meet someone new. Say hello, tell them your name, ask for their name, and say "Nice to meet you."

Hint: "Hello, my name is... What is..."

Scenario 2: At the Shop

You want to buy a pen. Say "Excuse me," ask for the price, and say "Thank you."

Hint: "Excuse me. How much is..."

Scenario 3: Asking for Directions

You are lost. Ask the teacher where the hospital is.

Hint: "Excuse me, where is..."
