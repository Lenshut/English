Speaking Tasks

Execute this formal speaking evaluation aloud to prepare for the test format.

Task: 7-Minute Formal Evaluation Simulation

Part 1: Introduction & Habits

Teacher asks: "Tell me about your daily routine."

Your Goal: Speak for 45 seconds using always/usually/never and conjunctions.

Part 2: The Past

Teacher asks: "Tell me a story about your last vacation."

Your Goal: Speak for 1 minute using was/were, -ed, irregulars, and didn't.

Part 3: The Future

Teacher asks: "What are your plans for the next five years?"

Your Goal: Speak for 1 minute distinguishing between concrete plans (going to) and dreams (will).

Part 4: Opinion & Defense

Teacher asks: "Do you think living in the city is better than the country?"

Your Goal: Defend your stance for 1 minute using "I think", comparatives, and "because". Ask "What about you?"

Reading

Read the Grading Rubric and Sample High-Scoring Response.

Grading Rubric for Speaking Test
• Tense Accuracy (25%): Does the student use past verbs for past events, and future verbs for future plans?
• Vocabulary Use (25%): Does the student use a variety of verbs, adjectives, and conjunctions (and, but, because)?
• Conversational Flow (25%): Does the student speak in full sentences? Do they use "And you?" to keep the conversation going?
• Pronunciation (25%): Are words clear? Are the -ed endings pronounced correctly?

Sample High-Scoring Response (Part 2: The Past):
"Last year, I went to London with my family. It was very beautiful, but it was also very expensive. We visited many museums and we ate delicious food. I didn't speak perfect English, but the people were very nice. What about you? Where did you go for your last vacation?"

Self-Check Questions:

1. Does the sample response use past tense verbs correctly?
2. What conjunctions does the sample use to improve flow?
3. How does the sample response pass the turn back to the teacher?
