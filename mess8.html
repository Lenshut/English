PLACEMENT TEST : LEVEL 8

LEVEL 8: PROFICIENCY

Evaluator Measurement & Placement Protocol

🗣️

I. Speaking (5 Prompts)

Instructions to Evaluator: Do not prompt or translate. Assess immediate verbal reaction.

1. Prompt: "State your overarching objective for a presentation on climate change using meta-discourse."
2. Prompt: "Steelman my argument that 'AI will destroy jobs', and then rebut it."
3. Prompt: "Identify and explain a cognitive bias that ruins business decisions."
4. Prompt: "Synthesize these two views: Perspective A wants to cut costs. Perspective B wants to improve quality."
5. Prompt: "Deliver a 30-second TED-style opening hook on the 'Death of Privacy' starting with 'Imagine a world...'."
📖

II. Reading (5 Questions)

"My overarching objective is to delineate the flaws in our methodology. To steelman the opposition's argument, they rely heavily on the sunk cost fallacy. However, what they conveniently omit is the catastrophic long-term ROI. Synthesizing these disparate views, a holistic evaluation reveals that the paradox of our success is creating an echo chamber."

6. What is the speaker's main goal (overarching objective)?

7. What logical fallacy is the opposition using?

8. What is the opposition "conveniently omitting"?

9. How is the speaker resolving the conflicting arguments?

10. What paradox does the holistic evaluation reveal?

✍️

III. Writing (5 Questions)

Write the correct sentence based on the instructions:

11. Meta-discourse: Let me [clarify] this point further -> Let me ______.

12. Synthesis: Combine two views: Synthesizing these [different] views -> Synthesizing these ______ views.

13. Rebuttal: You posit this is true, but what you ______ is the cost.

14. TED Hook: That is not science fiction; that is our current ______.

15. Thesis: The central thesis of my inquiry ______ that X leads to Y.

🧩

IV. Grammar (5 Questions)

16. What I am trying to convey ___ that the system is broken.

  • a) is
  • b) are
  • c) being

17. Let me play devil's advocate for a second. What if it ___?

  • a) failed
  • b) fails
  • c) failing

18. ___ synthesizing the data, a clear consensus emerges.

  • a) Upon
  • b) By
  • c) In

19. It is a paradox that ___ we connect, the more isolated we feel.

  • a) the more
  • b) most
  • c) more

20. Our first ___ is economic viability.

  • a) contention
  • b) content
  • c) intending
📚

V. Vocabulary (5 Questions)

21. To "delineate" means to:

  • a) Describe precisely
  • b) Delete
  • c) Confuse

22. The "sunk cost fallacy" involves:

  • a) Continuing due to past investment
  • b) Buying boats
  • c) Ignoring data

23. To "steelman" an argument is to:

  • a) Ignore it
  • b) Attack a weak version
  • c) Build the strongest version of it

24. "Disparate" views are:

  • a) Similar
  • b) Essentially different
  • c) Desperate

25. "Conjecture" refers to:

  • a) Hard facts
  • b) An unproven guess
  • c) A legal document

ANSWER KEY

I. Speaking

  1. "My overarching objective today is to delineate..."
  2. "To steelman your argument in good faith... however, my rebuttal is..."
  3. Must name a bias (e.g., Confirmation Bias) and accurately diagnose it.
  4. "Synthesizing these disparate views, a holistic picture emerges..."
  5. "Imagine a world where... That is not science fiction, that is our current reality."

II. Reading

  1. To delineate the flaws in the methodology.
  2. The sunk cost fallacy.
  3. The catastrophic long-term ROI.
  4. By synthesizing them via a holistic evaluation.
  5. Success is creating an echo chamber.

III. Writing

  1. elucidate
  2. disparate
  3. conveniently omit
  4. reality
  5. posits

IV. Grammar & V. Vocab

  • 16. a (is)
  • 21. a (Describe precisely)
  • 17. a (failed)
  • 22. a (Past investment)
  • 18. a (Upon)
  • 23. c (Strongest version)
  • 19. a (the more)
  • 24. b (Essentially different)
  • 20. a (contention)
  • 25. b (Unproven guess)

MEASUREMENT CONSIDERATION

Do not rely solely on the written scores. The speaking execution dictates the final placement.

Belongs in Level 7 (-1 Level)

If the student fails the steelman challenge by attacking a strawman, or cannot synthesize the disparate views without reverting to basic "and/but" summaries, they lack true C2 cognitive agility. Demote to Level 7 to focus on systemic deduction, abstract rhetoric, and high-level negotiation.

Belongs in Level 8 (0 Level | Baseline)

Absolute C2 Mastery. The student is an elite orator. They can instantly diagnose logical fallacies, deliver theatrical TED-style hooks on command, and maintain unassailable executive presence. Keep in Level 8 for the final Capstone project and Socratic Interrogation.
