Speaking • Level 4

Intermediate Speaking

Connect complex ideas. Practice using relative clauses to describe, master hypothetical conditions, utilize formal connectors, and perform structured professional interviews.

🔮 Unreal Present (2nd Conditional)

Answer the prompts aloud using 'If I had/were..., I would...'.

Task 1: If I won the lottery...

Speak for 45 seconds about what you would do with $10 million. Mention where you would live and what you would buy.

Task 2: If I were the President...

Name three laws you would change. Start with: "If I were the leader of my country, I would..."

🔗 Advanced Connectors

Explain your answer using 'Furthermore', 'Moreover', or 'On the other hand'.

1. Why is remote work good? (Use: Furthermore)
2. Compare city life vs country life. (Use: On the other hand)
3. Why should people exercise? (Use: Moreover)
4. Is technology helpful? (Use: Although)

💼 Embedded Questions

Transform direct questions into polite embedded questions (Do you know where... is?).

Direct: "Where is the post office?"
Polite: "Do you know where the post office is?"
Direct: "What time does the bank close?"
Polite: "Could you tell me what time the bank closes?"
Direct: "Who is the manager?"
Polite: "I was wondering if you know who the manager is."

👔 Interview Mastery

Answer these standard interview questions with structured paragraphs.

"What are your greatest strengths?"

Structure: Statement -> Relative Clause detail -> Example.
(e.g. "I am a person who learns quickly...")

"Tell me about a difficult situation you handled."

Use 'Although' and 'Therefore' to structure your story.

🤝 Negotiation & Concession

Handle this workplace scenario using 'While it is true that...'

Scenario: Budget Dispute

Your partner says the project is too expensive. Acknowledge their point but explain why the investment is necessary.

Hint: "While it is true that the cost is high, I believe the value is..."
