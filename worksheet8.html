2. Matching (60 Pairs)

Match the C2 concept or vocabulary word on the left with its definition on the right.

1. Posit A. Put forward as a basis of argument
2. Invariably B. In every case or on every occasion
3. Radical C. Affecting the fundamental nature of something
4. Bias D. Prejudice in favor of or against one thing
5. Echo chamber E. Environment where a person only encounters beliefs coinciding with their own
6. Steelman F. Building the strongest version of an opponent's argument
7. Strawman G. A weak or imaginary opposing argument
8. Paradox H. A seemingly absurd or self-contradictory statement
9. Counterintuitive I. Contrary to intuition or common-sense expectation
10. Utilitarian J. Designed to be useful or practical; maximizing total benefit
11. Deontological K. Based on rules and duty, regardless of outcome
12. Assume L. Suppose to be the case, without proof
13. Pivot M. The central point on which a mechanism turns
14. Aligned N. Place or arrange things in a straight line; agree
15. Tangential O. Diverging from a previous course or line
16. Albeit P. Although
17. Consensus Q. A general agreement
18. Spearhead R. Lead an attack or movement
19. Hesitation S. The action of pausing before saying or doing something
20. Inaction T. Lack of action where some is expected or appropriate
21. Status quo U. The existing state of affairs
22. Redefine V. Define again or differently
23. North Star W. A guiding vision or ultimate goal
24. Architecting X. Designing and making
25. Methodology Y. A system of methods used in a particular area of study
26. Robust Z. Strong and healthy; vigorous
27. Variables AA. Elements, features, or factors that are liable to vary
28. Jargon BB. Special words or expressions used by a profession
29. Demystify CC. Make a difficult subject clearer and easier to understand
30. Synthesize DD. Combine a number of things into a coherent whole
31. Holistic EE. Characterized by the comprehension of the parts as a whole
32. Overhaul FF. A thorough examination and repair/rebuild
33. Thereby GG. By that means; as a result of that
34. Cascading HH. Pouring downward rapidly and in large quantities
35. Perceptive II. Having or showing sensitive insight
36. Integrity JJ. The quality of being honest; structural soundness
37. Interject KK. Say something abruptly, especially as an interruption
38. Circle back LL. Return to a previous topic
39. Mediator MM. A person who attempts to make people involved in a conflict come to an agreement
40. Inextricably NN. In a way that is impossible to disentangle or separate
41. Systemic OO. Relating to a system, especially as opposed to a particular part
42. Proliferation PP. Rapid increase in numbers
43. Existential QQ. Relating to existence
44. Supersede RR. Take the place of; supplant
45. Predicated SS. Found or base something on
46. Sovereignty TT. Supreme power or authority
47. Homogenization UU. Making things uniform or similar
48. Insight VV. Deep understanding of a person or thing
49. Impromptu WW. Done without being planned or rehearsed
50. Thread XX. A theme or characteristic running throughout a situation
51. Represents YY. Be a symbol or embodiment of
52. Feasibility ZZ. The state or degree of being easily or conveniently done
53. Viable AAA. Capable of working successfully; feasible
54. Derail BBB. Cause a train to leave its tracks; obstruct a process
55. Synergy CCC. Combined action or operation
56. Incumbent DDD. Necessary for (someone) as a duty or responsibility
57. Sunk cost EEE. Money that has already been spent and cannot be recovered
58. Extrinsic FFF. Not part of the essential nature; coming from outside
59. Intrinsic GGG. Belonging naturally; essential
60. Socratic HHH. Relating to Socrates or his educational method of asking questions
