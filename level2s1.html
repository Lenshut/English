7

Reading

Read the passage carefully and answer the comprehension questions.

Meet Sarah. Sarah is a software engineer who lives in a bustling apartment in the heart of the city. Her daily routine is incredibly structured, starting very early in the morning. Every weekday, Sarah wakes up at exactly 5:30 AM. She doesn't like to sleep in because she enjoys the quiet hours before the city wakes up. First, she drinks a large glass of water and does yoga in her living room for thirty minutes. She believes that exercising in the morning gives her the energy she needs for the rest of the day.

At 6:15 AM, Sarah takes a quick shower and gets dressed for work. She usually wears comfortable but professional clothes. After getting ready, she goes to the kitchen to prepare breakfast. She doesn't eat a heavy breakfast; she usually just has a bowl of oatmeal with fresh berries and a cup of black coffee. She reads the morning news on her tablet while she eats.

By 7:30 AM, Sarah leaves her apartment and walks to the subway station. The train ride takes about forty-five minutes. During the commute, she listens to educational podcasts or reads a book. She arrives at her office at 8:30 AM and immediately checks her emails. She works hard all morning, attending meetings and writing computer code. She usually takes a short lunch break at 12:30 PM. She eats a salad or a sandwich at her desk.

Sarah finishes work at 5:00 PM. In the evening, she goes to the grocery store, cooks dinner, and relaxes. She often watches a documentary or calls her family. She never goes to bed late; by 10:00 PM, she is fast asleep. Her weekends, however, are completely different. She sleeps until 9:00 AM, meets friends for brunch, and rarely looks at her computer.

Questions:

1. What time does Sarah wake up on weekdays, and why?
2. What does Sarah do before she takes a shower?
3. Does Sarah eat a large breakfast? What does she eat?
4. How does Sarah travel to work, and what does she do during the commute?
5. How are Sarah's weekends different from her weekdays?
