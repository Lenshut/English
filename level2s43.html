Speaking Tasks

Practice reading rapid tense shifts aloud.

Task A: Read Aloud

"Yesterday I played tennis, today I am tired, and tomorrow I will rest."

Task B: Read Aloud

"I usually drink coffee, but yesterday I drank tea, and tomorrow I am going to drink water."

Task C: Read Aloud

"She didn't study last night, so she is studying right now, and she will take the test tomorrow."

Task D: Teacher Roleplay (The Time Spinner)
Teacher:
"Let's play Time Spinner! The verb is 'Eat'. The time is 'Yesterday'."
You:
"I ate a big pizza yesterday."
Teacher:
"Excellent. The verb is 'Go'. The time is 'Next year'."
You:
"I am going to go to London next year."
7

Reading

Read the time-traveling short story and map verbs to past, present, and future.

My Life Journey

Ten years ago, I was a student. I lived in a small town and I usually rode my bicycle to school. I didn't have much money, but I was happy. I studied very hard every day.

Now, I live in a big city. I am a manager at a large company. I always drive my car to work. My life is very busy, but I still love it. I sometimes miss my old town, but I prefer the city.

In the future, things will change again. Next year, I am going to buy a new house. When I am older, I will stop working and I will travel the world. I promise I will visit every continent!

Questions:

1. Ten years ago, how did the author usually go to school?
2. Ten years ago, did the author have a lot of money?
3. Now, where does the author live?
4. Next year, what is the author going to do?
5. In the future, what will the author do when they stop working?
