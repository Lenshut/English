7

Reading

Read the story about David's shopping trip and answer the questions below.

Hello! My name is David. Today is Saturday. I am not at school. I am at a big store with my mother and my sister, Lily. We want to buy new clothes today. The store is very big and it has many clothes.

First, I look at the shirts. I see a blue shirt and a red shirt. I like the blue shirt. I ask the man in the store, "Excuse me, how much is the blue shirt?" The man says, "The blue shirt is fifteen dollars." I say, "Thank you." I want to buy the blue shirt. Remember, a shirt is singular, so we say "is".

Next, my sister Lily looks at the pants. She likes the black pants. Pants are plural in English. We have two legs, so pants take an "s". Lily asks, "Excuse me, how much are the black pants?" The woman says, "The black pants are twenty dollars." Lily likes the pants.

Then, my mother looks at shoes. Shoes are plural too, because we have two feet. My mother wants black shoes. She asks, "How much are the black shoes?" The man says, "The shoes are thirty dollars." My mother buys the shoes.

We are very happy. I have a shirt, Lily has pants, and my mother has shoes. We go home and eat dinner. Shopping for clothes is very fun!

Questions:

1. Where is David today?
2. How much is the blue shirt?
3. Why are pants plural in English?
4. How much are the black pants?
5. What does the mother buy?
