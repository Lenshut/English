Speaking Tasks

Read the directions aloud clearly. Use hand gestures for straight, left, and right. Then, roleplay.

Task A: Read Aloud

"Go straight. Turn left. Turn right."

Task B: Read Aloud

"Excuse me. Where is the store? Go straight and turn right."

Task C: Read Aloud

"Do not turn left. Go straight to the park. It is on the right."

Task D: Teacher Roleplay
Teacher:
"Excuse me, please. Where is the hospital?"
You:
"Go straight and turn left. The hospital is on the right."
Teacher:
"Turn left and then turn right?"
You:
"No, do not turn right. Turn left. It is on the right side."
7

Reading

Read the story about Mark asking for directions in his town and answer the questions below.

Hello! My name is Mark. Today, I am in my town. I want to go to the big store to buy apples. I walk on the street. I see a tall man. I say, "Excuse me, please. Where is the big store?" The tall man smiles. He says, "Go straight. Do not turn left. Turn right at the tall tree. The store is on the right." I say, "Thank you very much!" The man says, "You're welcome."

I go straight. I walk fast. I see the tall tree. I stop at the tree. I do not turn left. I turn right. I look to the right. I see the big store! The store is on the right. I walk to the store and I buy sweet red apples.

After the store, I want to go to the park. I see a nice woman. I say, "Excuse me. Where is the park?" The nice woman says, "Turn left at the hospital. Go straight. The park is on the left." I say, "Thank you!" I turn left at the hospital. I go straight. I see the park on the left. I play in the park. It is a good day.

Questions:

1. Where does Mark want to go first?
2. What does the tall man say? (Go straight and turn...)
3. Is the store on the left or on the right?
4. Where does Mark turn left for the park?
5. Is the park on the left or on the right?
