6

Speaking Tasks

Practice reacting to situational problems aloud using 'will'.

Task A: Read Aloud

"The phone is ringing. I will answer it!"

Task B: Read Aloud

"You look very tired. I will carry that bag for you."

Task C: Read Aloud

"It is raining outside, and you have no umbrella. I will drive you home."

Task D: Teacher Roleplay (The Hero Game)
Teacher:
"Oh no! I am so hungry, but I don't have any food."
You:
"Don't worry, I will buy you a sandwich!"
Teacher:
"Thank you! But now it is very cold in this room."
You:
"I will close the window and I will give you a jacket."
7

Reading

Read the dialogue scenarios and identify the spontaneous 'will' decisions.

At the office:
Anna: "Oh no, the printer is broken and I need these papers."
Ben: "Don't worry, Anna. I will fix it for you."

At home:
Mom: "Dinner is ready, but we don't have any drinks on the table."
Sam: "I will get the water and the glasses, Mom!"

At the cafe:
Tom: "I forgot my wallet! I can't pay for my coffee."
Jerry: "It's okay, Tom. I will pay for your coffee today. You can pay next time."
Tom: "Thanks! I promise I will pay you back tomorrow."

Questions (What is the decision/promise?):

1. What will Ben do for Anna?
2. What will Sam get for his mom?
3. What is Tom's problem?
4. What will Jerry do for Tom?
5. What does Tom promise to do tomorrow?
